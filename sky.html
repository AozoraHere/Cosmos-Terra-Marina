Exploring the Atmosphere and Its Phenomena

Welcome to the special page that will discuss everything about the sky!

1. Definition of Sky

The atmosphere is Earth's protective shield that blocks deadly solar radiation and provides oxygen. The sky looks blue due to Rayleigh Scattering. When sunlight hits atmospheric gases, blue light—which has shorter wavelengths—is scattered in all directions, making the daytime sky appear bright blue.

2. Sky Phenomena

Our sky often puts on magical natural light shows. Here are some of them:

A. Aurora

Dancing green and reddish-purple curtains of light at the poles, created when solar winds collide with Earth's magnetic field.

B. Solar Halos

A beautiful rainbow-like ring around the sun, caused by light refracting through ice crystals in high cirrus clouds.

C. Fire Rainbows (Circumhorizontal Arc)

A rare phenomenon where clouds glow with rainbow colors. It happens only when the sun is very high and its light passes through flat ice crystals.

3. Sky Classification

Clouds are classified into different types based on their shapes. Here are three major clouds you should know:

Cumulus Clouds

Fluffy, cotton-like clouds that usually appear during bright, clear weather.

Stratus Clouds

Flat, gray, and layered clouds that cover the sky like a blanket, bringing gloomy or overcast weather.

Cumulonimbus Clouds (The Storm King)

Giant tower-shaped clouds that are highly dangerous, as they produce severe weather, heavy rain, lightning, and tornadoes.
